What is Praetor VR?

And why it's called 'Praetor'

PRAETOR VR a VR Headset made by students, for the students. It is a modular platform for engaging AI enabled virtual experiences running on a 3D engine in Python. Our solution is designed from the ground-up to be affordable, customizable, and easy to use!

While 'Praetor' isn't really an English term, it's derived from Latin and roughly means 'the one who goes before'. Only 1-3% of the global population is a regular user of Virtual Reality. Let's bring that up, shall we? Be the one who goes before!

What is our aim?

And what we will do

Aims of the project:
• To provide easy to use modular VR hardware.
• To raise the profile of AI integrated in VR.
• To understand the current 3D rendering with AI and its extended capabilities with VR.
• Increase affordability of said hardware.
• Increase accessibility for users with disabilities.

Features of Praetor VR

What sets this project apart from others

Community

Make it easier to acquire, easier to use. Built for students and tinkerers, but just as robust and implementable as other VR solutions!

Software

Use the VR applications we create, or make your own! Supports Steam VR, and other VR ecosystems. Custom simulations coming soon!

Customizability

Praetor systems are modular- both hardware and software along with functional and cosmetic mods. Make it your own.

Affordability

Look up the cost of desktop VR headsets. About INR 70,000 as a starting price. Praetor VR systems cost only INR 10,000 to 15,000.

Inclusivity

User interface being powered by AI motion tracking and gestural interfaces to improve accessibility for people with disabilities.

Source code

For now, the project is closed source. But all source code (hardware and software) will be open sourced on the first stable release!

We have several VR ecosystems like SteamVR, Oculus Quest and the rest. What is different about Praetor VR is the pricing- the affordability and the user interface being powered by AI motion tracking and gestural interfaces to improve accessibility for people with disabilities. What distinguishes this project is that while none of the other solutions are cost-sensitive- and certainly no solution has inclusivity for people with special needs- that is what I concentrated on, for everyone deserves immersive education. We are entering a changing era of education, and democratizing such technologies is just the next step in educational innovation.
